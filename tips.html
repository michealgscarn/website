Resources

We wanted to share some of our favorite tips that have helped us over the years. These suggestions are what have worked for our program, and we encourage you to modify these to fit your program best.

Notebook

Make Templates
To create a consistent entry layout, we make templates of our most used projects. We first create a list of the most important things to accomplish in each entry. We divide those list items into categories to create a good flow and organization in our entries. Then we create our outline on a separate, clean piece of paper. We have the project title at the top of the page and a placeholder subtitle beneath the title. Next, we start developing the outline based on the categories and list items we made earlier. We have our categories as headers and list items as items beneath the headers. Sometimes, we create sample sentences to add for even more consistency within entries.

Overall, creating templates for your entries is a great way to improve ORGANIZATION and CONSISTENCY while reducing the stress of writing in the notebook.

VEX Forum
The VEX Forum is a great resource and communication center for teams. You can find everything from coversation threads to robot design discussions. We use the VEX Forum to keep up with all of the new designs and notebook tips teams and students have shared. We recommend teams check this platform out to keep up with VEX related conversations.

vexforum.com
Robot Events
Robot Events is a great website for teams. This website allows users to view a wide variety of information about everything VEX. Teams can use Robot Events to sign up for events, view the global skills rank, participate in online challenges, and view event results. We often use this to sign up and view events as well as keep tabs on the global skills ranking. We recommend this website for teams to access everything VEX Robotics.
