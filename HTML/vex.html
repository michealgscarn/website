What Is VEX

Our Team

We are a group of PLTW students who attend North Hardin High School in Radcliff, Kentucky. We engage ourselves in VEX Robotics in order to enhance our knowledge in various fields of engineering. Our VEX Robotics program takes place at the Early College and Career Center in Elizabethtown inside of the engineering labs. We currently have three teams that compete in the VEX Robotics competition being 4104A, 4104B, and 4104C. We also share our VEX Robotics program with Central Hardin and John Hardin High School who is mainly coached by Jason Neagle. Using our knowledge of the design process, our teams will attempt to design and build a robot that is capable of winning the VEX Robotics competition.

For the sake of the future of our Vex Robotics program, we plan on trying to excel in Vex Robotics in every way possible by becoming experts in the engineering process, know what it is like to be an engineer, and try to learn from our failures and our victories. We also try to introduce the sport of Vex Robotics to others to try to help create potential engineers and help them find something they never knew they wanted.

We engage ourselves in Vex Robotics every day in order to improve our live skills of teamwork, problem solving, and perservierance as well as enhancing pur knowledge in the various fields of engineering and computer science. Our VEX Robotics program takes place in our house where we build, program, document, and drive our robot. We as well try to support the teams we compete against in competitions as much as we can.
