Outreach Activities

We love giving back to our community and encouraging students to continue learning about STEM. Our favorite part of participating in the VEX Robotics program is getting to help students who are eager to learn more and try new things. Engaging these students offers them a potentially life changing opportunity and a road to a successful college education and career.

It is so important to us to inspire future engineers because similar encouragement has shaped who we are today. We are so thankful for our mentors and coaches who have given up their time to inspire us to work hard in everything that we do. Without these important people, we would not be who we are today. It is very important to us to offer this kind of encouragement to students who are just like we were just a couple years ago.

Covington Catholic Robotics Camp
We collaborated with the Covington Catholic VEX students to help campers build, program, troubleshoot their robots. For 5 days, 30 elementary to middle school students worked in teams to build and program their robots. At the end of the week, the teams participated in a special competition with their robots and program. Our team and Covington Catholic teams showcased the robots that our teams built for the VRC "Turning Point" season to inspire the campers to persue VEX in the future.

Notebook Presentation
We gave a presentation on our top 5 tips for starting an engineering notebook for a VEX Robotics Competition. Despite the restrictions caused by COVID-19, we collaborated with the Milford Robotics program to share how our team approaches the engineering notebook. 110 members from 16 schools attended the virtual meeting.
